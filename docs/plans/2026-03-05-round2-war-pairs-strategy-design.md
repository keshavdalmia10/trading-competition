## Risk Management

### Stop-Loss Framework

| Position Type | Stop-Loss | Rationale |
|---------------|-----------|-----------|
| War Longs (defense/energy) | -15% | High conviction, give room |
| War Shorts (airlines/consumer) | -25% (stock rises 25%) | Moderate tolerance |
| Flexible Longs | -12% | Momentum - cut fast if trend breaks |
| Flexible Shorts | -20% | Same logic, slightly more room |

### Position-Level Exit Triggers
- Stop-loss hit: immediate exit
- Thesis break: exit within same session
- Target reached (30%+ gain): take partial profits (sell half)

### Portfolio-Level Risk Rules
- Max net exposure: 60% in either direction
- Max single-sector exposure: 35% of total buying power
- Portfolio drawdown hits -15% from peak: reduce all positions by 30%

### Key Calendar Dates
- Mar 17-18: FOMC meeting
- Mid-March: US-China trade chief meeting
- Ongoing: CrowdStrike and other earnings
- Any day: Ceasefire/escalation news

## Contingency Plans

### Ceasefire
1. Immediately close all war shorts
2. Trim war longs by 50%
3. Rotate freed capital into flexible sleeve (recovery plays)

### Escalation (oil >$100)
1. Add to energy longs, especially OXY
2. Add to airline shorts
3. Shift flexible sleeve more bearish

### Tariff Escalation
- Short multinationals harder
- Add to domestic small caps

### China Deal Surprise
- Rotate flexible longs into semis/tech

### Fed Surprise Cut (unlikely)
- Close financial longs
- Add growth
